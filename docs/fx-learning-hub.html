// WHAT IS FX CHART PATTERN TRADING?

Chart pattern trading means recognizing recurring shapes on a price chart that historically predict where price will move next. When you combine these patterns with proper stop loss (SL) and take profit (TP) levels, you create a rules-based trading system.

The 3 Pillars of Pattern Trading:
1. Pattern Recognition — Spot the shape forming on the chart
2. Entry Trigger — Wait for confirmation before entering
3. Risk Management — Set SL below/above pattern, TP at measured move

Candlestick Basics
Before patterns, you must read individual candles. Each candle = 1 time period of price movement.
💡 A candlestick has 4 prices: Open, High, Low, Close. The body shows Open–Close. The wicks show High–Low.

Bullish Candle

Green/White. Close is HIGHER than Open. Buyers won this period. Good sign.

Bearish Candle

Red/Black. Close is LOWER than Open. Sellers won this period. Bad sign for bulls.

Doji

Open ≈ Close. Indecision. Neither buyers nor sellers are in control. Watch for reversal.

Hammer

Long lower wick, small body at top. Strong bullish reversal signal at the bottom of a downtrend.

// KEY CANDLESTICK PATTERNS

🔨 Hammer / Hanging Man

Long lower wick (2–3x body). At bottom of downtrend = Hammer (bullish). At top of uptrend = Hanging Man (bearish). Enter after next candle confirms.

⭐ Engulfing Pattern

Second candle completely "engulfs" the first. Bullish Engulfing at support = strong buy. Bearish Engulfing at resistance = strong sell.

✨ Morning / Evening Star

3-candle pattern. Morning Star = bullish reversal (down, doji, up). Evening Star = bearish reversal (up, doji, down).

📌 Shooting Star / Inverted Hammer

Long upper wick. At top of uptrend = Shooting Star (bearish). At bottom = Inverted Hammer (bullish). Wait for confirmation.

🕊 Doji Types

Standard Doji, Dragonfly (long lower wick), Gravestone (long upper wick). All signal indecision and potential reversal when at key levels.

🌅 Marubozu

Full body candle with NO wicks. Very strong momentum. Bullish Marubozu = very strong buying pressure. Bearish = very strong selling.

BEARISH REVERSAL

Head & Shoulders

Three peaks — the middle (head) is highest. Enter SHORT when price breaks below the neckline. Very reliable reversal signal after uptrend.

TPHead height projected down

BEARISH REVERSAL

Double Top (M Pattern)

Two peaks at same level — price couldn't break higher twice. Sell when price breaks below the valley between the two peaks (neckline).

SLAbove the two tops
TP= Height of the M
Win Rate~65%
BULLISH REVERSAL

Double Bottom (W Pattern)

Two troughs at same level. Price bounced twice from support. Buy when price breaks above the peak between the two bottoms (neckline).

SLBelow the two lows
TP= Height of the W
Win Rate~67%
BULLISH CONTINUATION

Ascending Triangle

Flat top resistance with rising lows. Buyers getting more aggressive. Buy the breakout above the flat top with increased volume.

SLBelow last swing low
TP= Triangle height
Win Rate~72%
BEARISH CONTINUATION

Descending Triangle

Flat bottom support with falling highs. Sellers getting more aggressive. Sell the breakdown below the flat support line.

SLAbove last swing high
TP= Triangle height
Win Rate~69%
BULLISH CONTINUATION

Bull Flag

Strong upward "pole" followed by a tight sideways/downward "flag" consolidation. Buy the breakout above the flag's upper trendline.

SLBelow flag low
TP= Pole height from breakout
Win Rate~65%
NEUTRAL — WATCH

Symmetrical Triangle

Converging trendlines — lower highs and higher lows. Could break either way. Trade the breakout direction with volume confirmation.

SLOpposite side of breakout
TP= Triangle width

Bear Flag

Strong downward "pole" followed by a tight sideways/upward "flag" consolidation. Sell the breakdown below the flag's lower trendline.

SLAbove flag high
TP= Pole height from breakdown
Win Rate~63%
BEARISH REVERSAL

Rising Wedge

Both support and resistance lines slope up but converge. Despite rising price, momentum is weakening. Sell when price breaks below the lower trendline.

SLAbove wedge high
TP= Wedge start height
Win Rate~66%
BULLISH REVERSAL

Falling Wedge

Both support and resistance lines slope down but converge. Selling pressure is declining. Buy when price breaks above the upper trendline with volume.

SLBelow wedge low
TP= Wedge start height
Win Rate~68%
BULLISH CONTINUATION

Cup & Handle

U-shaped "cup" followed by a small downward "handle" consolidation. Buy the breakout above the cup's rim (resistance). Common in longer timeframes.

SLBelow handle low
TP= Cup depth from breakout
Win Rate~71%

// RISK RULES EVERY TRADER MUST FOLLOW

📏 1% Rule

Never risk more than 1–2% of your account on a single trade. Losing 10 trades in a row (which happens!) would only lose 10% of your account, not blow it up.

⚖️ Risk:Reward Ratio

Minimum 1:2 ratio. If you risk 30 pips, target at least 60 pips profit. At 50% win rate with 1:2 RRR, you still make money overall.

🛑 Always Set Stop Loss

NEVER trade without a stop loss. The market can move 200+ pips against you in minutes during news events. A stop loss is your insurance policy.

📊 Journal Every Trade

Record: entry reason, pattern, entry price, SL, TP, result. After 50 trades, analyze your data to see which patterns work best for YOU.

FX Trading Glossary
Complete beginner's reference — every term you'll encounter.

PIP

Smallest price movement in FX. For EUR/USD: 1 pip = 0.0001. For USD/JPY: 1 pip = 0.01. Your profit/loss is measured in pips.

LOT SIZE

Standard lot = 100,000 units = ~$10/pip. Mini lot = 10,000 units = ~$1/pip. Micro lot = 1,000 units = ~$0.10/pip. Start with micro lots.

STOP LOSS (SL)

An order that automatically closes your trade at a loss if price moves against you. Placed at a level where your pattern is "proven wrong."

TAKE PROFIT (TP)

An order that automatically closes your trade in profit at your target price. Set using the pattern's "measured move" technique.

SUPPORT

A price level where buying pressure has historically stopped price from falling further. The "floor" of price. Look to BUY near support.

RESISTANCE

A price level where selling pressure has historically stopped price from rising further. The "ceiling" of price. Look to SELL near resistance.

BREAKOUT

When price moves decisively through a support or resistance level. Breakouts often signal the start of a new trend. Wait for candle CLOSE beyond the level.

TREND

Uptrend = series of higher highs and higher lows. Downtrend = lower highs and lower lows. Trade WITH the trend for higher probability setups.

SPREAD

Difference between bid and ask price. Your trade starts slightly in negative. EUR/USD typical spread = 1–2 pips. Affects very short-term trades most.

LEVERAGE

Ability to control large position with small capital. 1:100 leverage = $100 controls $10,000. Amplifies both profits AND losses. Use carefully.

MARGIN

Deposit required to open a leveraged position. With 1:100 leverage, to open 0.1 lot EUR/USD (10,000 units) you need ~$100 margin.

CONFLUENCE

When multiple signals align at the same price (pattern + support + RSI oversold). The more confluence, the higher probability the trade will work.

NECKLINE

Key level in H&S, double top/bottom patterns. The critical support/resistance line that, when broken, confirms the pattern and triggers the trade entry.

MEASURED MOVE

Technique to set TP. Measure the height of the pattern (e.g. head to neckline) and project that distance from the breakout point. That's your target.

TIMEFRAME

M1 (1min) to MN (monthly). H4 and Daily are best for beginners — fewer false signals, more reliable patterns, less noise and stress.

DEMO ACCOUNT

Practice account with virtual money. Always trade on demo for at least 3 months before using real money. Treat it exactly like real money.

🥇 XAU/USD — GOLD

Gold priced in US Dollars. 1 standard lot = 100 troy oz. 1 pip = $0.10/lot. Gold moves 1,000–3,000 pips/day. Use wider SL (200–500 pips). Highly sensitive to USD strength, inflation, and geopolitical risk.

🥈 XAG/USD — SILVER

Silver priced in USD. 1 standard lot = 5,000 oz. 1 pip = $0.01/lot. Much more volatile than Gold — can move 3,000–8,000 pips/day. Smaller account? Use 0.01 micro lots. Follows Gold but amplified.

🛢 WTI / BRENT OIL

Crude oil contracts. 1 pip = $0.01. Moves driven by OPEC decisions, US inventories (Wed 10:30 EST), geopolitics. WTI = US benchmark. Brent = global benchmark. Typical daily range: 200–800 pips.

📊 COMMODITY PIP GUIDE

Gold: SL 200–500 pips typical. Silver: SL 500–1500 pips. Oil: SL 100–300 pips. Always use smaller lot sizes for commodities than FX — they move much more in dollar terms per pip.
